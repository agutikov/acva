# S9.5.4 — Neural vocoders (HiFi-GAN, BigVGAN, WaveNeXt)

**Phase:** P9.5 (TTS expression)
**Estimate:** 1 week
**Status:** stage placeholder.
**Shape:** investigation only.

## Context

Explicit follow-up from the planning conversation. Many
modern TTS stacks split synthesis into a mel-spectrogram
generator (Tacotron / VITS / etc.) and a vocoder that
turns mel into waveform. HiFi-GAN was the 2020 reference;
BigVGAN and WaveNeXt are more recent, with better
naturalness.

The question is whether acva benefits from running its own
vocoder layer (e.g. swap Speaches' default vocoder for
BigVGAN) or if the engine-bundled vocoders are good
enough.

## Tasks

### T9.5.4.1 — Quality survey

A/B test the same mel-spectrogram through:
- HiFi-GAN (reference)
- BigVGAN-base
- WaveNeXt
- Whatever Speaches / Kokoro / Piper ships internally

Same source, same room conditions, blind rating across 20
samples. Capture in `docs/reports/`.

### T9.5.4.2 — Latency cost

Per-vocoder mean / P95 RTF (real-time factor) on the dev
RTX 4060. Anything > 0.3 RTF is unviable for streaming TTS.

### T9.5.4.3 — Integration cost

Speaches' vocoder is internal — swapping requires either a
PR upstream or a fork. Document the cost; we likely defer
unless quality lift is dramatic.

### T9.5.4.4 — Decision

Closure note in this stage:
- "engine-bundled vocoders are good enough" — most likely.
- "ship a vocoder swap path" — only if T9.5.4.1 shows a
  clear win.
